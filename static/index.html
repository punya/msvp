Mapping Sexual Violence is a website that emerged out of an Out of Print initiative to produce an anthology featuring fiction and non-fiction writing on sexual violence in South Asia. The editors of the special issue – Meena Kandasamy, Samhita Arni and Indira Chandrasekhar – recognised that this effort needs to extend beyond the literary community. Meena proposed the idea of a map that records stories of sexual violence from across the country and the region. The purpose, to provide a platform to document silent voices, voices that have been unable to share their stories.

Mapping Sexual Violence is intended to be a collective resource. It is meant for its users to use as a platform to share their stories, to build a network resource and a community.

We want to acknowledge the contributions of Punyashloka Biswal, who has so generously donated his time to create this website, and Mira Brunner and Rob Singleton for their critical ideas and input on safety, accessibilty and design. Gratitude, also, to numerous other friends, conversations with whom helped hone ideas and create this place.

  • This website is an open and safe space for all people to participate. Please be mindful of what you post. Any posts that contain hate speech, libelous or defammatory statements will be removed by the moderator.
  • All posts will first be approved by a moderator before appearing onsite.
  • No naming of perpetrators. This may sound contrary to our purpose, but we want this website to first and foremost be a place where all can share their stories and responses. But we also need to protect this space and do not wish for the sharing of stories to be usurped by trials in public, nor do we wish to be embroiled in law suits.
  • No using photographs.

Please be aware of your safety at all times. If you are undergoing sexual violence or harassment, please do consider if posting on this website can create further difficulties for you before posting. We also allow untagged posts if you wish to protect your location – we strongly encourage you to do this if you feel unsafe.

You may also consider zooming out and posting on a city, or a district, instead of at a street or location.

Please do not compromise your safety.

The only data collected by this website is the location where you tag your post, and the contents of your post itself.
